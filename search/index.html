Tips & Tricks

Is Viasat Browser your default browser?

If so, great! If not, click here to learn how to change it.

Miss your old bookmarks?

Find out how to import them to Viasat Browser here.

Play Six Degrees of Bacon

Type an actor’s name followed by "bacon number" (ex. Will Smith bacon number) in the address bar to get the number of actors needed to connect that actor to Kevin Bacon.

Restore Tabs

Accidentally closed a tab you needed or accidentally closed the browser? Restore tabs by typing Ctrl+Shift+T (⌘⇧T for Mac). Hit it again to get the next to last tab, and so on.

Viasat Browser Extensions

Did you know you can add all sort of cool extensions to Viasat Browser? Visit the Chrome store to add new tools to personalize your browser!

Play Zerg Rush

Type zerg rush in the address bar and red O’s will begin to fall. Use the "o" key to move the falling O’s.

Is your browser up to date?

Most browsers automatically update themselves after being closed. So remember to fully close (not minimize) Viasat Browser every once in a while.

Page Zoom

Want to make page content larger or smaller? Press Ctrl (⌘ for Mac) and the "+" or "-" key to quickly zoom in or out of any web page and change the size of text or other on-screen elements.

Address Bar Utilities

The address bar at the top of your browser is good for more than typing in URLs. Try typing questions like how many feet in a mile? or find the lowest cost flight from Boston to Denver.

Switch Between Tabs

You can switch between your open tabs by typing Ctrl+Tab (⌃+Tab for Mac).

Incognito Mode

Want to prevent Viasat Browser from communicating with the ViaSat Hinting Service? Ctrl+Shift+N (⌘⇧N for Mac) will open a window in incognito mode. Note that in this mode, Viasat Browser speed optimizations are not available.

Translate Pages

Want to translate web pages from one language to another? Load the Google Translate extension and click on the icon to translate.

Chromecast

Do you want to use Chromecast? Just click on the ︙ in the top right and select "Cast..." to send video streams directly to your TV via a Google Chromecast device.

Try some fun effects!

Just for fun, type do a barrel roll in the address bar. Like that? Now try typing askew.

Full Screen

Quickly switch between full screen and normal view by hitting F11.

Go back a page

Just press Alt+Left Arrow (⌘← for Mac).

Grammar Extension

Want a cool extension that checks your grammar? Try the Grammarly extension and never again accidentally use "your" instead of "you’re".

Search by Image

Want to know where a picture came from? Hold the "S" key and right click an image to do a reverse image search in Viasat Browser.

Less is More

Want to type less? Enter the "site name"+Ctrl+Enter (⌘↵ for Mac) and the www and .com are automatically added for you.

Play Atari Breakout

Take a brain break! Go to Google, type in Atari Breakout, click on "Images," and have fun!

Bookmark Shortcut

Found a web page you like? Press Ctrl+D (⌘D for Mac) to save the current page as a bookmark.

Set a Timer

Did you know that you could set a timer by typing "Set a timer for X sec" (or minutes or hours)? You don’t need to stay on that tab; the timer continues as long as the tab remains open.

Calculator

Did you know that you could do arithmetic by just typing in your calculation? Try typing 123+456 to see a full calculator displaying the answer.

Switch Between Tabs

Want to quickly move between tabs? Type Ctrl+Tab or Ctrl+Shift+Tab (⌘⌥→ or ⌘⌥← for Mac).

Mute a Tab

Prevent audio on new tabs from interfering with your browsing by right-clicking on the tab and selecting "Mute tab".

Race a T-Rex

Race dinosaurs through the desert with the hidden dinosaur game. When your Internet is out and you try to visit a webpage, Viasat Browser displays an offline game featuring a cute T-Rex.
